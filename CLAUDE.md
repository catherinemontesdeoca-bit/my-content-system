# Aircall Content Design System

## Brand Voice

Aircall's voice is **friendly and approachable**. We talk to users like a knowledgeable colleague — warm, clear, and never condescending. We make complex things feel simple.

**We are:**

- Warm but not over-the-top
- Clear but not blunt
- Helpful but not patronizing

**We are not:**

- Robotic or overly formal
- Casual to the point of being unprofessional
- Vague or wishy-washy

---

## Writing Rules for UI Copy & Microcopy

### Voice & Tone

- Write in second person: use "you" and "your"
- Use active voice: "Save your settings" not "Your settings have been saved"
- Be direct: get to the point quickly

### CTAs (Buttons & Links)

- Format: verb + outcome
- ✅ "Start free trial" "Add a number" "Invite your team"
- ❌ "Click here" "Submit" "OK"

### Error Messages

- Acknowledge what happened
- Explain why (if helpful)
- Tell the user what to do next
- Never blame the user
- ✅ "We couldn't connect your number. Check your internet connection and try again."
- ❌ "Error 404. Invalid input."

### Empty States

- Explain what the space is for
- Give the user a clear next action
- ✅ "No calls yet. Make your first call to get started."

### Tooltips & Helper Text

- Keep it to one sentence when possible
- Front-load the most important information
- Avoid starting with "This" or "This is"

### Capitalization

- Use sentence case everywhere (including buttons and headings)
- ✅ "Add team member" ❌ "Add Team Member"

### Punctuation

- No periods at the end of buttons, labels, or headings
- Use periods in full sentences (error messages, helper text, empty states)

---

## Words to Avoid

|Avoid|Use instead|
|---|---|
|Please|(just say what to do)|
|Sorry|(acknowledge and fix)|
|Invalid|Not valid / incorrect|
|Failed|Couldn't / wasn't able to|
|Abort|Cancel|
|Kill|Stop / end|

---

## When Reviewing Copy, Always Check:

1. Is it in active voice?
2. Does the CTA have a verb + outcome?
3. Is it in sentence case?
4. Would a friendly colleague say this out loud?
